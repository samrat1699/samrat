About me

I recently completed my Bachelor of Science in Statistics and am currently working as a research fellow at the ARI (Advanced Research & Innovation) where I contribute to ongoing research projects in biostatistics, data analysis, and applied statistical modeling.

I am passionate about advancing my knowledge and research skills, and I plan to pursue a PhD in Statistics or related fields. My current work focuses on applying statistical methods and computational tools to real-world datasets, and I am particularly interested in areas such as machine learning, causal inference, and bioinformatics.

I am committed to learning, collaborating on research projects, and contributing to high-quality, reproducible research. I am excited to develop my skills further and make meaningful contributions to the field of statistics and data science.

Prevalence and Determinants of Malnutrition Among Women of Reproductive Age in Urban and Rural Bangladesh

Samrat Kumar Dev Sharma; Dr. MD Tareq

September 2025 – Present Jagannath University
Malnutrition among women of reproductive age (WRA) poses a serious public health challenge in Bangladesh, where both undernutrition and overnutrition are prevalent. This dual burden negatively impacts not only women’s own health and productivity but also the health of future generations.
 
 
 
 
 

Breast Cancer Detection with Explainable AI: Grad-CAM and Bounding Box- Based Interpretability

Samrat Kumar Dev Sharma

November 2019 – August 2020 Jagannath University
Breast cancer detection using mammographic images is a vital application of AI in medical diagnostics, but the lack of interpretability in deep learning models limits clinical adoption. This study integrates Grad-CAM heatmaps and bounding box visualization to enhance transparency and reliability in AI-driven detection.
